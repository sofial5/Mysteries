2014-03-08

"Flight 370 of Malaysia Airlines"

Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 was a regular flight that departed from Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, bound for Beijing, China, on March 8, 2014. On board the Boeing 777-200ER were 227 passengers and 12 crew members. Approximately one hour after takeoff, while the aircraft was over the South China Sea, radar contact and communications with the aircraft were lost. This triggered an extensive international search effort to locate the missing plane. Investigators determined that the aircraft had changed course deliberately and had flown in the opposite direction for several hours after losing contact. This revelation led to expanding the search areas to the Indian Ocean, west of Australia. Despite the most extensive search efforts in aviation history, involving multiple countries and advanced resources, the plane and its occupants were never found.

Last Seen

South China Sea, northeast of the Malaysian peninsula.

Currently, the case of Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 remains an unresolved mystery. Despite intensive search efforts by multiple countries and the use of advanced technology, the plane and its occupants have never been found. Investigations have suggested that the plane changed course deliberately and continued flying for several hours after losing contact, but it has not been conclusively determined what caused its disappearance or exactly where it ended up. The case has generated numerous theories and speculations, but to date, no conclusive evidence has been found to explain what happened definitively.

2007-05-03

Madeleine Beth McCann

Madeleine McCann, a three-year-old girl from Leicestershire, a county in England, disappeared from the Portuguese holiday resort of Praia da Luz in the Algarve on May 3, 2007; a case still unresolved and still the subject of intense interest among the public and the press. Kate and Gerry McCann, parents of Madeleine McCann, released a statement on the Find Madeleine website on May 3, 2023, marking the latest anniversary of their daughter's disappearance. They reiterated their hopes of being reunited with her someday and shared a poem, "The Contradiction," written by Clare Pollard, to express their feelings.

1945-12-24

The Sodder Family

The Sodder family consisted of George and Jennie Sodder and their ten children. In the early hours of December 24, 1945, a fire completely destroyed their home. While five of the children managed to escape, the remaining five who were sleeping on the second floor disappeared and were never found. Despite extensive searches and various theories, including the possibility of abduction, no conclusive evidence about the fate of the missing children was ever discovered. During the initial hours and days after the fire, efforts were made to locate the children within the rubble of the burned house, but no human remains corresponding to them were discovered. This led to speculation and theories as to whether the children may have been abducted or if they somehow escaped undetected. The case of the Sodder family remains an unsolved mystery that has sparked numerous theories and speculations over the years.

The Sodder Family House

Fayetteville, West Virginia, United States.

The last update in January 2022, the Sodder family case remains an unsolved mystery. Over the years, there have been various theories and speculations about what happened to the five children who disappeared after the 1945 fire. Some theories suggest they may have been abducted, while others propose they perished in the fire in a way that their remains were not identified. Continued efforts by the Sodder family, private investigators, and interested individuals have not yielded new conclusive evidence to solve the mystery. The case continues to be remembered as one of the most famous unsolved mysteries in American history.
